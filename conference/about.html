The Veterans Emergency Management Evaluation Center (VEMEC) will hold its fourth annual conference in the DC metropolitan area (venue TBD) September 13-14, 2012.

ARC conference provides a unique forum for VA and non-VA professionals representing private and public sectors in the Emergency Management (EM) field to network, share, and learn through:

  • • Exploring and evaluating new concepts
  • • Fostering research that leads to best practices
  • • Discussing cross-cutting issues and shared challenges
  • • Exploring and discovering innovative collaborative tools
  • • Evaluating technology driven and simple solutions in EM

Designed for practitioners, policy makers, community leaders and other major stakeholders, the VEMEC annual ARC conference (formerly known as Comprehensive Emergency Management Program Evaluation and Research Conference-CEMPER) provides an opportunity for the participants to gain knowledge on research, training, and resources available in various EM phases.

Past conference speakers have represented national and local organizations dedicated to develop, evaluate, and adopt the best practices in the EM field, a select sample of which include:

  • • Federal agencies- FEMA, ASPR, CDC
  • • Local health departments- Seattle Cook County, District of Columbia
  • • Local health departments- Seattle Cook County, District of Columbia
  • • Private firms- FedEx, MESH, Rx Response
  • • Academia- Georgetown University, University of California– Los Angeles, Yale
  • • Not for Profit- RAND Corporation

The conference will also include a variety of presentations in format, including:

  • • Selected abstracts; Poster sessions; Presentations; Breakout sessions
